What is SAT and who takes it?

SAT was earlier known as Scholastic Aptitude Test but as of today, it does not stand for anything. SAT is a test taken by students aspiring to do their Bachelors in US, Europe, Singapore and a few other countries. The test is for almost 4 hours.

Score and basic structure of SAT

SAT is scored out of 1600. It is divided into two basic components – Math, Verbl. Each of these two components is scored out of 800, thereby totaling 1600. Most of the questions are multiple-choice. A person gets 1 point for a right question and there is no penalty for answering the question incorrectly.

Services Regular Question

Math

This section is divided into two parts – without calculator comprising of 20 questions (25 min) and with calculator comprising of 38 questions (55min). That’s a total of 58 questions in 80 min. Topics consist of Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry (including trigonometry and co-ordinate geometry) Function and graphs and Data Interpretation. A total of 13 questions are not multiple choice.

Verbal

This section has two parts – Reading, and Writing & Language.

Reading

A total of 52 questions to be answered in 65 min. This is more than reading comprehension. There are 5 passages and a couple of them consist of Graphs and Charts and questions based on the figures given.

Writing

A total of 44 to be answered in 35 min. There will be 4 passages and questions are based on finding the mistakes or improvement of the passage. This is more like making corrections to a draft.

Essay

In SAT essay, one has to read the essay and analyze the essay. The essay is rated on three parameters - Reading, Analysis and Writing – on a scale of 0 to 4 on each parameter. Two graders rate it and the total score is out of 24 marks.

What is ACT and who takes it?

ACT is quite popular in the US and the number of ACT test takers surpassed those of SAT test takers in 2012. People who are good in Math generally prefer ACT to SAT. The four sections are English, Math, Reading and Science. These sections are scored individually on a scale of 1–36, and a Composite score is provided which is the whole number average of the four scores. The average time per question is less in ACT than that of SAT.

Services Regular Question

English

this section has 75 questions which have to be answered in 45 min. There will be 5 passages and each passage constitutes 15 questions. The questions vary from improving sentences to paragraphs. This section tests not only on grammar but also on writing style.

Math

60 questions in 60 min. Syllabus for ACT can be more than that of SAT. A few topics such as logarithm, matrices, limits etc. are included in ACT. In general, the questions are quite straight forward when compared to SAT.

Reading

this section constitutes 4 passages and each passage has 10 questions and has to be answered in 35 min. The passages are not difficult and unlike SAT, it does not have dual passages. The passages are of different genre such as one from prose, humanities, natural science, social science etc.

Science

this section will have 5 to 7 experiments and will have a total of 40 questions and have to be answered in 35 min. Prior science knowledge is not required. It is more of data interpretation and one should be able to analyze the data give. Thos who have taken science will relate to this section better.

Essay

the essay section is optional and has to be written in 40 min. A topic is given along with 3 perspectives. One has to elaborate on the perspectives given and one has to give one’s perspective as well. It could be in sync with the perspective given or different.

What is SAT Subject test (SAT II) and who takes it?

SAT subject tests are not mandatory for most universities. There are a few universities though which make these tests Mandatory (depending on the choice of one’s UG major). There are a few universities which ‘consider’ these tests – which means that those students may have an edge if they have taken SAT subject tests. If one is looking at taking admission in the top universities then it is recommended that one takes SAT subject tests.

Unlike SAT, SAT subject tests are 60 min. The number of questions depends on the subject one chooses. This test is scored out of 800. A student can choose the subject of his her choice, depending on Undergraduate course degree he/she prefers. A good score in this test helps a student in getting scholarship and the chance of getting a seat in a good college increase.

SAT subject tests can be taken for Math (Level 1 and 2), Physics, Chemistry, Biology (Ecology/Molecular), English Writing, English Literature, US History, World History, and various languages such as Spanish, French etc.

Score above 700 may seem good but in SAT subject tests, a score of 700 is not good enough to get into good universities. For example, a score of 700 in SAT Math level 2 is 48 percentile. (A score of 800 is 81 percentile). One should aim at getting a perfect score in SAT subject test and is it not difficult if one is focuses and takes a lot of practice tests.
